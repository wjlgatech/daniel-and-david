## LinkedIn — primary launch post
**Attach:** `dd-launch-capability-ladder.png` (or a screenshot of the app)

It's live. My sons and I just shipped a free thing for families.

For two years I've been raising my boys — Daniel (11) and David (6) — to be **wise, creative,
AI-ready builders**, in public, on GitHub. Today it's something any family can actually *use*.

It's a free app (no signup, installs to your phone, works offline, your notes never leave your
device). It walks your family through one **Builder Loop**: a four-week project run as **5 fast
cycles** — notice a real problem → build something small → **show it to a real person** → learn
what flopped → try again, a little better. Finding what *failed* is the win.

The ladder we climb has no dollar signs on it: notice → ask → make → **serve** → learn → team →
bless. AI may change work dramatically. Our kids don't need to fear it — they need to become
*wiser* than it.

It's free for everyone. I'd love your eyes on it, and your kid's.

## LinkedIn — the Founding Families post (honest scarcity)
**Attach:** `dd-launch-founding-halving.png`

A rare privilege, on a fair schedule.

We're opening the project to a small number of **founding families** — and, like Bitcoin's
issuance, the founder's reward **halves** at each epoch while the cohort **doubles**: Genesis is
**8 families** (1×), then 16 (½×), 32 (¼×), 64 (⅛×) — and after that the whole thing is free and
open to everyone.

The earliest families take the most risk and do the most work, so they earn the most honor:
they co-design the curriculum and are named in the project forever. It's real scarcity — we
actually stop each epoch at its cap — and it doubles as the best lesson I can give my boys about
how scarce, fairly-issued value really works.

**Genesis is open now: 8 spots.** It's an application, and a mutual fit — we're choosing each
other. For parents, guardians, and educators (adults only).
