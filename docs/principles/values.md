# Principle: Values — the lines we never cross

Skills make you capable. Values make you trustworthy. **Trust is the real moat** — in
business and in life. These are the lines we hold no matter what the scoreboard says.

## The why under all of it

This family builds because we believe we were made to create, to serve, and to bless — to
take part, in our small way, in building the Kingdom of God. Wealth is a **tool and a test**:
a tool for doing good at scale, and a test of whether we'll stay honest, humble, and generous
when we have it. Everyone is welcome to build here, of every belief; this section simply
states the *why* honestly, because a builder should always know what they're building for.

## The non-negotiables

1. **Tell the truth.** No false claims, no fake affiliations, no inflated numbers, no
   tricks. Venture #1's "do-not" branding list exists because we'd rather make less money
   honestly than more money by deceiving people. A reputation is built over years and lost
   in a sentence.

2. **Obey the law and keep your word.** Permits, taxes, contracts, compliance gates — all of
   it, even when it's slower and costlier. "We didn't think we'd get caught" is never a
   reason. A promise made is a debt owed.

3. **Serve, don't exploit.** Sell people things that genuinely make their lives better.
   Never prey on fear, addiction, or ignorance. If you'd be ashamed to sell it to your
   grandmother, don't sell it.

4. **Protect the vulnerable.** Especially children. Consent before photographing anyone's
   kids. Guard customer data like it's your own. Power is for protecting people, not using
   them.

5. **Give as you grow.** Generosity is built in from rung 1, not deferred to "someday when
   I'm rich." Tithe, give, mentor, open-source. Money held with an open hand blesses many;
   money clutched tight rots the one holding it.

6. **Stay humble.** Success is partly skill, partly grace, partly luck, and partly the
   thousands of people whose work you stand on. Pride is the most expensive thing a builder
   can buy. Stay teachable. Credit others loudly; blame yourself first.

7. **Honor people over profit.** Customers, partners, contributors, teammates, family — real
   humans, never just numbers in a dashboard. If a choice forces money against people, choose
   people. The money is replaceable; trust and relationships are not.

## The test for any decision

Before any big move — a price, a product, a marketing claim, a partnership — ask:

> 1. **Is it true?** (Would it survive full daylight?)
> 2. **Is it legal and within my word?**
> 3. **Does it genuinely serve the person on the other side?**
> 4. **Would I be proud to explain it to my kids — and to God?**

If any answer is no, don't do it. There's always another way to win that passes all four.

## The promise this makes possible

A builder who is known to be honest, lawful, generous, and humble becomes someone others
*want* to work with, invest in, and buy from. The values aren't a tax on success — over a
long enough time, **they are the engine of it.** Doing it right and doing it well turn out to
be the same road.

For Daniel and David: if you remember nothing else, remember this — **be the kind of person
your word can be trusted on, and use whatever you build to bless other people.** Everything
else is details.
