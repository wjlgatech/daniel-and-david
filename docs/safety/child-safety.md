# Child Safety

Children are first-class participants here — a six-year-old's drawing is a real contribution.
That is beautiful, and it creates real responsibility. These are the **hard rules.** They are
not negotiable, and they apply to every person and every AI agent in this project.

## The operating rules

```text
1.  Children do not create public profiles.
2.  Parents or approved educators submit contributions on behalf of younger children.
3.  Use first names, aliases, or project names — never full identity details.
4.  No public location, school, contact details, schedules, or travel plans — ever.
5.  No direct messages between adults and children.
6.  No adult–child matching without parent or educator mediation.
7.  All public child contributions are reviewed before publication.
8.  No AI tool may collect, retain, or expose a child's personal data by default.
9.  Every child-facing activity carries a clear adult-supervision note.
10. Any safety concern stops the work until a responsible adult has reviewed it.
```

## Why these exist

This project imagines a global hub where kids, parents, mentors, and AI agents collaborate.
That is exactly the environment where, without design, children can be exposed. Designing the
limits **first** is what makes the open, generous version safe enough to build.

## Supervision note (applies to every Builder Loop activity)

Every [Builder Loop](../builder-loop/) step that touches a real person, a real place, or an AI
teammate assumes a **present, responsible adult.** "Talk to three real people," "give it to one
real person," and "ask the AI" are all *adult-supervised* steps.

## Reporting

If you see anything that risks a child's safety — in an issue, a PR, a contribution, or an AI
output — stop and follow [`SECURITY.md`](../../SECURITY.md) to reach a maintainer privately.
Do not debate it in public. Safety concerns are always welcome and never punished.
