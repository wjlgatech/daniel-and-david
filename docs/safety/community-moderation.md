# Community Moderation

This project optimizes for **quality and safety, not volume.** Every public contribution —
especially anything from or about a child — is **reviewed before it appears.**

## The review gate

```text
Contribution submitted
   → automated checks (links, toolkit, no secrets — see scripts/check.sh)
   → human review: is it true, kind, safe, and child-appropriate?
   → child-safety check: no personal data, no contact details, no location?
   → published   (or)   sent back with a kind, specific reason
```

Nothing about a child goes public on the strength of automation alone. A human looks first.

## What reviewers check

- **True.** No unsupported claims presented as fact. (Use the
  [5W1H grid](../curriculum/critical-thinking/).)
- **Kind & child-appropriate.** Language, images, and topics fit a six-year-old reader. See
  [`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
- **Safe.** No personal data, contact details, location, school, or schedule
  (see [child-safety](child-safety.md)).
- **Honest about AI.** If AI helped, it's allowed — but the contribution shouldn't misrepresent
  who did what (see [ai-use-boundaries](ai-use-boundaries.md)).

## Scope of contributions (for now)

While the project is small, contributions open around **tightly scoped issues** — clear,
reviewable, kind. We deliberately are **not** chasing "100 contributions a day" yet; that
volume goal creates moderation and safety risk while children are involved. See the
[Theory of Change](../vision/theory-of-change.md) and the [Hub guide](../community/hub.md).

## Moderators

Maintainers (see [CODEOWNERS](../../.github/CODEOWNERS) where present) are the moderators. A
contribution can be held or removed for safety at any time; the reason is always given kindly
and specifically. Disagreements go to a [Discussion](../../CONTRIBUTING.md), not an edit war.
