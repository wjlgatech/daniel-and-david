# Theory of Change

A mission tells you *where* you're going. A theory of change tells you *how the work causes
the outcome* — and what you'll measure to know it's working. This is the causal spine under
the [mission](mission.md).

> **The claim we are testing:** if a family runs small, real [Builder Loops](../builder-loop/)
> — noticing problems, asking better questions, building with AI as a teammate, serving real
> people, and telling the truth — then over time the child grows in agency, judgment,
> AI-wisdom, and service. We measure the child's **behavior change**, not our ambition.

---

## The causal model

```text
INPUTS  (what we provide)
   Family guides · real mini-ventures · AI scaffolds · the values framework · the 5W1H grid
        │
        ▼
BEHAVIORS  (what the child actually does)
   Notices problems · asks better questions · attempts independently first ·
   uses AI as support, not a crutch · tests ideas on real people · reflects honestly
        │
        ▼
NEAR-TERM OUTCOMES  (weeks to months — observable)
   More curiosity · clearer explanations · stronger persistence ·
   better AI-verification habits · better communication with real people
        │
        ▼
LONG-TERM OUTCOMES  (years — who they become)
   Agency · ethical judgment · entrepreneurial capability ·
   service orientation · responsible use of technology
```

Each arrow is a **bet**. If a near-term outcome isn't moving, the bet above it is wrong and we
change the inputs — that's what the "What Worked / What Failed / What We Changed" reflection at
the end of every loop is for.

---

## The North Star metric

> ### Independent Builder Evidence per child per month

A child earns **one evidence point** each time they *independently* demonstrate one of these:

- explain a concept in their own words;
- identify an unsupported claim (and say what evidence would change their mind);
- run a small real customer/interview conversation;
- design a simple test of an idea;
- build a useful artifact;
- revise something *because* of feedback;
- use AI and then **independently reproduce or explain** the key work;
- choose honesty over a misleading shortcut.

This is deliberately a measure of **capability**, not participation. It is much better than
counting GitHub stars, number of documents, number of agents, number of prompts, or theoretical
revenue. (See the [Builder Loop scorecard](../builder-loop/) for how a single loop is scored.)

### What we are **not** optimizing for (yet)

- ❌ "100 contributions a day" as a near-term goal — see why in the [Hub guide](../community/hub.md).
- ❌ Public child profiles, child–adult matching, or open uploads before the
  [safety layer](../safety/) is in place.
- ❌ Revenue milestones as the lead metric. The [money ladder](milestones.md) is a *later*
  venture track — a compass, not the near-term score.

## The near-term proof goal

Before scaling anything, we want a small, honest proof that the loop changes behavior:

```text
10 pilot families
   → 3 completed Builder Loops each
   → ~20 documented child artifacts
   → 10 parent reflections
   → 5 improved lessons
   → 3 external contributors
```

The first real milestone is **not** "100 contributions a day." It is:

> **"Ten families completed one real Builder Loop, and their children can independently explain
> what they learned."**

That is the evidence that earns the next stage.

See the [Builder Loop](../builder-loop/), the [curriculum](../curriculum/), and the
[safety layer](../safety/) for how the inputs are actually delivered.
