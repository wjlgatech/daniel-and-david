# The Milestone Ladder

A compass, not a promise. Each rung is less about the number and more about the **new skill
the number forces you to learn.** You cannot fake your way up — each level requires a real
capability the last one didn't.

| # | Rung | Target | Horizon | The skill it forces | The question it answers |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 🌱 Seed | **$1,000,000** | Year 1 | Ship *one* real product people pay for | "Can I create value at all?" |
| 2 | 🌿 Sprout | **$10,000,000** | Year 2 | Make it *repeatable* — a system, not luck | "Can I do it on purpose, again?" |
| 3 | 🌳 Tree | **$100,000,000** | Year 3 | *Leverage* — teams, capital, distribution | "Can I build through other people?" |
| 4 | 🌲 Forest | **$1,000,000,000** | Year 5 | Build an *institution* that outlives me | "Can I create something durable?" |
| 5 | ⛰️ Mountain | **$10,000,000,000** | Year 7 | Move an *entire market* | "Can I bend the world toward good?" |

## How to read this ladder

- **The horizon is aspiration, not a deadline.** Some rungs take longer. Climbing *at all*
  is the win; the order and the lessons matter more than the calendar.
- **Each rung is a different game.** Rung 1 is about *demand* (will anyone pay?). Rung 3 is
  about *leverage* (can you build through others?). Skills don't transfer for free — you
  re-learn at every level. That's the point.
- **Cash is the scoreboard; capability is the score.** A venture can generate $1M and teach
  you almost nothing if it was luck. A $10k venture can teach you everything if you
  understand exactly *why* every dollar came in.

## Rung 1 starts now — and it's real

We are not starting with a fantasy. **Venture #1 is live in this repo:**
[`ventures/kc-matchday-basecamp/`](../../ventures/kc-matchday-basecamp/). A legal,
venue-partnered local-commerce business with real economics, real customers, and real
compliance gates.

> $1M in year one will not come from one $29 kit. It comes from learning the *engine* —
> demand, margin, distribution, repeat — on a small venture, then pointing that same engine
> at bigger and bigger problems. Venture #1 is where you first feel the engine turn over.

## The rule that governs every rung

At **every** level, before we celebrate a number, we ask:

1. Did we serve people honestly? (No tricks, no false claims, no harm.)
2. Did we obey the law and keep our word? (Especially the compliance gates.)
3. Are we giving as we grow? (Generosity is built in from rung 1, not deferred to rung 5.)

A number that fails those three questions doesn't count. We'd rather climb one rung the
right way than five the wrong way.

## A note on the very top

$1B and $10B are not the finish line. They are the point at which your decisions affect
thousands of families, whole markets, maybe whole nations. If you ever get there, the
mission is unchanged from rung 1: **build something good, do it honestly, and use it to
bless.** Just at a scale where the blessing reaches very, very far.
