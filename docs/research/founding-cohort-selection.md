# Research — selecting a small founding cohort (fair, predictive)

*Synthesis behind [founding-families](../founding-families.md) selection rubric. Reuse before re-researching admissions/cohort selection.*

## Key findings
- **What predicts follow-through** — **role construction** (parent sees growing the child as *their* job — #1 Hoover-Dempsey predictor) + **self-efficacy** + felt **invitation**. Completion RCT: parent *readiness to change* is the sole predictor of attendance; attrition concentrates in the first 2 weeks.
- **Non-cognitive predictors** — **conscientiousness** is the most-replicated (β≈.20 across 267 samples) > grit (modest, overlaps conscientiousness, can proxy privilege) > growth mindset (weak as a *screen*, good as culture). **SDT autonomous motivation** ("genuine pull") predicts completion; controlled motivation (FOMO/résumé) predicts dropout.
- **How selective cohorts admit** — YC: **determination** > intelligence; **application = commitment device**; Edmund Hillary Fellowship & On Deck select for **contribution to the group** (Adam Grant: one taker degrades an 8-person cohort); Deep Springs: fit/self-direction/service, not GPA. **Entrofy**: two-stage (qualify on a minimum bar, then compose for diversity) so merit & diversity don't trade off.
- **Ethics/legal** — fair, inclusive, **mutual-fit** framing. Do **not** select on: wealth/income, parents' credentials, child's grades/school/name, religion, ZIP, household structure, "portfolio" (proxies for protected classes / access). Faith-**informed**, not faith-**required**. COPPA: adults apply, no child PII.
- **Rubric** (1–3 each, threshold then compose): role construction · autonomous motivation · follow-through history (conscientiousness) · giver orientation · builder comfort · honest capacity · mutual fit. Behavioral questions (e.g. "a commitment you almost quit") beat self-flattery; **honest uncertainty is a plus**.
