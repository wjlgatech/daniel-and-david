# Daniel's Track — age 11, the Builder

Hey Daniel. 👋 You're old enough to build real things that real people pay for. This track
turns you into a *builder* — someone who can take an idea, an AI team, and a few dollars and
make something that works. We learn it on a live business: [KC Matchday
Basecamp](../../../ventures/kc-matchday-basecamp/).

Work through the quests in order. Each one ends with **something real you made or measured.**

> 🛠️ **New here? Run the [Builder Loop](../../builder-loop/) first** — four weeks of **5 fast
> build-show-learn cycles** (fast · frequent · failing-forward) that tie these quests to one real
> problem and real people. An adult should skim the [safety rules](../../safety/) before you start.

---

## Level 1 — The Engine of Value

### Quest 1.1 — Find a real problem
Walk around an event, a store, a busy night. Write down **5 problems** real people have
("my phone died," "I got rained on," "I couldn't find food"). For each, ask: *would someone
pay $5 to make this go away?* → Output: a list of 5, with your best guess at #1.

### Quest 1.2 — Understand margin (the most important number)
Take one product from venture #1, the **$29 Rain + Phone Rescue kit**. It costs about
**$7** to make. Compute:
- Profit per kit = price − cost = `$29 − $7 = ?`
- Margin % = profit ÷ price = `? ÷ 29 = ?%`
→ Output: the two numbers, in your own handwriting. *This is the heartbeat of every business.*

### Quest 1.3 — Read the scoreboard
Open the venture's [conservative July-3 scenario](../../../ventures/kc-matchday-basecamp/SPEC.md).
Find: revenue, costs, and profit. Explain to Dad in one minute *why* profit isn't the same
as revenue. → Output: the one-minute explanation.

## Level 2 — Ship Something Real

### Quest 2.1 — Price a product
Pick a real thing you could sell this month (a kit, a service, a digital thing). Set its
price using margin (Quest 1.2). Justify the price in 3 sentences. → Output: product + price + why.

### Quest 2.2 — Build with an AI teammate
Open [`agents/hello-agent/`](../../../agents/hello-agent/). Run it. Then change it to do
something *you* want. Read every line you change — you have to be able to explain it.
→ Output: your modified agent + a sentence on what it does. (See [agentic
engineering](../../principles/agentic-engineering.md).)

### Quest 2.3 — Make a real page
Open [`apps/web/public/index.html`](../../../apps/web/public/). Change the headline, add a
section, fix a color. Open it in a browser and see your change live. → Output: your edit,
opened in a browser, shown to someone.

---

## Level 3 — Meet the Market

### Quest 3.1 — Sell one real thing
With Dad's help and inside the [rules](../../principles/values.md), actually sell **one
thing** to **one real customer.** Watch their face. Did they hesitate? Why? → Output: the sale
+ one thing you learned from their reaction.

### Quest 3.2 — Measure it
Track 10 attempts: how many people you asked, how many said yes. That ratio is your
**conversion rate.** → Output: the number, and one idea to improve it. (This is exactly what
the venture's [analytics](../../../ventures/kc-matchday-basecamp/SPEC.md) measure.)

### Quest 3.3 — Improve and repeat
Change one thing (price, words, who you ask). Measure again. Did conversion go up or down?
→ Output: before/after numbers. *Now you've run an experiment — the core skill of every
AI-native builder.*

---

## Level 4 — Leverage (the big unlock)

### Quest 4.1 — Direct a team of agents
Use an AI agent to draft 3 things for venture #1 (a sign, a partner message, a guide entry).
**Edit each one** for truth and tone before it's allowed to exist. → Output: 3 drafts + your
edits, with a note on what you changed and why.

### Quest 4.2 — Teach David something
Pick one thing you learned and teach it to your little brother so he gets it. Teaching is the
test of understanding. → Output: David can explain it back.

### Quest 4.3 — Give some away
When you earn your first real profit, decide *before you spend any of it* how much you'll
give, and to whom. Then do it. → Output: the gift, given. (See [values](../../principles/values.md).)

---

## How you know you're leveling up

You're becoming a builder when you can: **spot a real problem → price a solution with margin
→ ship it with an AI teammate → sell it to a real person → measure it → improve it → and give
some of the profit away.** That whole loop, on your own. That's the engine. Everything bigger
is just the same engine pointed at bigger problems.

Go build something good, Daniel. 🛠️
