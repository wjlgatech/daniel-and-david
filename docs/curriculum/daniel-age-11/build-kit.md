## Your toolbelt

Each tool is a teammate. You're the captain. Here's what each is *for* — in one line — and where to
go deeper.

| Tool | What it's for (your words) | The one rule |
|---|---|---|
| 🧑‍💻 **Codex** (your $20/mo coding pair) | Build apps, agents, and fixes by describing what you want. | Read every line you keep; be able to explain it. |
| ⚙️ **Hermes** (on [`/free-llm`](../../../README.md)) | An always-on AI teammate that can even improve itself — running on **free** brains, so it costs ~$0. | It's a teammate, not the boss. You decide. |
| 🎯 **`/goal-10x`** | Hand it a messy goal; it finds the real next step and drives it to "green." | Green means the *check* passed — not "it looks done." |
| 🤖 **`/anyagent`** | Describe an app idea → it builds, grades, and improves a real agent app. | You approve before anything ships. |
| 🧠 **`/super-u`** | Turn anything you want to learn into a **skill you can do by hand** (with a worksheet). | Do the by-hand worksheet — that's the learning. |
| 🌐 **`/dreammaketrue`** | Turn a video/article/book into a **knowledge map** you can explore (even "talk" to the author's grounded avatar) and a post you can publish. | Grounded only — if it can't cite the source, don't trust it. |

*New tool? Ask it "what can you do?" and try the smallest thing first.*

## Your solo Builder Loop (with the kit)

The [Builder Loop](../../builder-loop/) is still the engine — you just run it yourself now, faster,
with the team:

1. **Pick** the smallest next thing to build this cycle (your idea first).
2. **Build it rough** — pair with **Codex / Hermes**; use **`/anyagent`** for an agent app,
   **`/goal-10x`** to drive a goal to green. *Your first attempt before the AI's.*
3. **Show one real person** (a present adult helps if it's in the real world).
4. **Verify the AI** — find one mistake it made and fix it. **Learn** — write the one lesson.
5. **Log it** in your [iteration log](../../builder-loop/iteration-log.md), and loop again.

## The Remote-Mentor Loop — Build & Show → Feedback

When Dad's away, the relationship doesn't stop — it goes **async**. You **build and show**; he gives
**feedback and support.** (This stays just between you and your parent — see
[safety rule 5](../../safety/child-safety.md).)

**Your "Show" (send after a cycle):**
```
What I built: ______________________________
Who I showed it to + what happened: ________
What failed / the one lesson: ______________
One AI mistake I caught: ___________________
Where I'm stuck / what I need from you: ____
(attach a 60-second screen recording or a photo)
```

**Dad's "Feedback" (back to you):**
```
🌟 What's strong: __________________________
🔧 One thing to try next: __________________
❓ One question to make you think: _________
✅ Green light? ___ (ship it / iterate / talk live)
```

Short, fast, frequent — like the loop itself. A 60-second show beats a perfect report you never send.

## Selling in the real world (toys, drinks, AI-powered products) — plan now, sell *together*

The crowd is the best teacher there is — and worth doing right. So the rule while your mentor is
away is simple: **you _plan_ the sell now; the actual public selling happens when a trusted adult is
physically with you** (when Dad's home, or another trusted adult who can be there with you). Public
selling is **never** a solo or AI-only activity — [safety rule 9 + the supervision
note](../../safety/child-safety.md).

**What you can do *now* (solo, with your AI team):**
- Pick the products (toys, drinks, AI-powered products) and **price them with margin + a reason**.
- Build the sign, the one-line pitch, and the checkout with **Codex / Hermes**.
- **Research the rules** (write down what you find for the adult to confirm): drinks and any food
  often need permits or have cottage-food rules; and follow every "do-not" in the
  [venture spec](../../../ventures/kc-matchday-basecamp/SPEC.md) — no protected brands/logos, no
  "official," no restricted-area selling.
- **Practice** the pitch on family and record a 60-second [show](#the-remote-mentor-loop--build--show--feedback) for Dad.

**What waits for the feast (an adult present):**
- The real sale to real customers — save it for when you're **together**, then go to the crowd and
  sell **side by side.** That's the best part, and it's worth the wait.

**Rules that keep it good and safe (now and then):**
- 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 A present adult for any public selling — always.
- 💳 **Never store anyone's card.** Use a real checkout (e.g. Stripe).
- 💛 **Serve, don't exploit; tell the truth** ([values](../../principles/values.md)).
- 🎁 **Give as you grow** — decide before you spend it how much of your profit you'll give.

## When you're together (the feast 🍽️)

The remote loop is the *daily*. When Dad's home is the *feast*: hours to **brainstorm and build
together**, then go out to the crowd and **sell side by side.** Save your hardest questions and
biggest ideas for that time — the in-person hours are for the things that don't fit in a 60-second
show.
