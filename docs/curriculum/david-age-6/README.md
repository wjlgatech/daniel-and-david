# David's Track — age 6, the Apprentice

Hi David! 🌟 You are six, and you are already a builder. This track is made of **little
adventures** you do with Mom, Dad, or Daniel. Each one is fun, and each one teaches you a
secret that grown-up builders use.

*Grown-up helper: read each adventure out loud and do it together. Keep it playful. The goal
is joy + one big idea per adventure.*

> 👪 **Grown-ups:** these adventures fit inside the [Builder Loop](../../builder-loop/) — the
> four-week family loop. Please read the [safety rules](../../safety/) first; every adventure
> here assumes you're doing it together.

---

## ⭐ Adventure 1 — Count the Treasure
Get a small pile of coins. Count them out loud. Now split them into **two jars**: one says
**KEEP**, one says **GIVE**. Put a few coins in GIVE.
> 💡 **Big idea:** Money is for *sharing*, not just keeping.

## ⭐ Adventure 2 — What Do People Need?
Walk around with a grown-up. Point at **3 things people need** (a drink when it's hot, an
umbrella when it rains, help carrying something).
> 💡 **Big idea:** A good business *helps people get what they need.*

## ⭐ Adventure 3 — Make the Sign
Venture #1 needs signs! Draw a colorful sign that says **"Game-Night Essentials"** with a big
arrow. Use your best colors.
> 💡 **Big idea:** You can *make* something a real business uses. You're on the team!

## ⭐ Adventure 4 — The Friendly Greeter
Practice saying, with a big smile: **"Hi! Do you need a phone charge or a rain poncho?"**
Say it to Mom, to Dad, to the mirror.
> 💡 **Big idea:** Being **kind and friendly** is the builder's superpower.

## ⭐ Adventure 5 — Trade and Smile
Set up a tiny pretend shop. "Sell" a cup of water for one coin. Say **"thank you!"** every
time. Count your coins at the end.
> 💡 **Big idea:** When you help someone, they say *thank you* — and sometimes thank-you is a coin.

## ⭐ Adventure 6 — One, Ten, Hundred
Stack 1 coin. Then 10. Imagine 100! Big things are just **lots of small things** stacked up.
> 💡 **Big idea:** Big stuff is made of small stuff. Start small. Keep stacking.

## ⭐ Adventure 7 — Say Thank You to God
At bedtime, say thank you for one thing you **made** today and one thing someone **gave**
you.
> 💡 **Big idea:** We build to say **thank you** and to **bless** other people. 🙏

---

## A note for grown-ups

David's whole curriculum is really four seeds: **count** (numbers are friendly), **create**
(I can make real things), **kindness** (serving people is the point), and **generosity**
(we give as we grow). Plant those at six, and the rest of the [milestone
ladder](../../vision/milestones.md) has soil to grow in.

Great job, David! You're a builder. 🌟
