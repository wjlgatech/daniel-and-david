# The Six Detective Words — for David (age 6) 🕵️

Hi David! A good detective doesn't just *guess* — they ask **questions**. There are six
magic question-words that crack any mystery wide open. Learn them, and you can figure out
almost anything!

*Grown-up helper: read each word aloud, do the little game, and celebrate every answer. The
goal is to make asking questions feel like a fun superpower.*

## 🔦 The Six Detective Words

| Word | It hunts for... | Practice question |
|---|---|---|
| 👤 **WHO** | the *people* | "Who is this for?" |
| 📦 **WHAT** | the *thing* | "What is it?" |
| 🗺️ **WHERE** | the *place* | "Where does it happen?" |
| ⏰ **WHEN** | the *time* | "When does it happen?" |
| ❓ **WHY** | the *reason* | "Why does it matter?" |
| 🛠️ **HOW** | the *way* | "How does it work?" |

Say them in order, fast, like a magic spell: **Who — What — Where — When — Why — How!**

## ⭐ Game 1 — Detective on a Snack
Pick any snack (say, an apple). Ask the six words about it:
- **Who** grew it? **What** is it? **Where** did it come from? **When** do we eat it? **Why**
  is it good for you? **How** did it get to our kitchen?
> 💡 **Big idea:** Six little words taught you a LOT about one apple!

## ⭐ Game 2 — Detective on Venture #1
Our family business sells a **rain poncho** so people don't get wet at the game.
- **Who** needs it? (someone in the rain!) **What** is it? **Why** would they buy it?
> 💡 **Big idea:** Good businesses *answer the six words* about what they sell.

## ⭐ Game 3 — The "Why" Chain
Pick anything and ask **"Why?"** Then ask "Why?" about the answer. Then again! See how many
"why"s you can climb.
> 💡 **Big idea:** **Why** is the most powerful detective word. Keep asking it.

## ⭐ Game 4 — Detective Says Thank You
At bedtime, be a detective about your day: **What** was the best part? **Who** were you kind
to? **How** can tomorrow be even better?
> 💡 **Big idea:** Asking good questions makes you wiser *and* kinder. 🙏

## A note for grown-ups

David is learning that **questions are tools**, not just things teachers ask. The six words
are the seed of real critical thinking — the same grid Daniel uses on business decisions and
the same one our AI teammates use before they act. Plant the habit of *asking before
believing* now, and everything in the [milestone ladder](../../vision/milestones.md) gets
easier later.

Great detective work, David! 🕵️⭐
