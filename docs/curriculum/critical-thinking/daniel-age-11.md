# The 5W1H Thinking Tool — for Daniel (age 11) 🧠

Daniel — this might be the most valuable single page in the whole repo. Coding and selling
are skills you can hire or automate. **Thinking clearly is the one you must own yourself.**
5W1H is a tool that forces clear thinking on demand. Master it and you'll out-think people
twice your age.

Keep the [grid](README.md) open while you work the quests.

## How to use the tool

Point the six words at any **target** — a claim, a plan, a product, a headline, a decision —
and answer the questions honestly. You don't need all 48 every time; grab the 3–4 that bite
hardest for *this* target. The goal is to surface what you'd otherwise miss: hidden risks,
forgotten people, untested assumptions.

---

## Level 1 — Learn the Tool

### Quest 1.1 — Interrogate an everyday object
Pick something nearby (your phone, a shoe). Walk all six words across it, writing one answer
each. → Output: 6 answers. Notice how much you now "see" that you didn't before.

### Quest 1.2 — Spot the strongest question
For a decision you're facing this week, which *one* of the 48 questions is the most
uncomfortable to answer? → Output: that question + your honest answer. **The question you want
to skip is usually the important one.**

## Level 2 — Think Like a Builder

### Quest 2.1 — 5W1H on Venture #1
Point the grid at the **$29 Rain + Phone Rescue kit** ([venture #1](../../../ventures/kc-matchday-basecamp/)):
- **Who** is most directly affected / harmed? **What** is the worst-case scenario? **Where**
  is the most need? **When** is the best time to sell? **Why** hasn't someone already done it?
  **How** could it harm us or others?
- → Output: one real risk and one real opportunity the grid surfaced that wasn't obvious before.

### Quest 2.2 — Kill a bad idea on purpose
Invent a *deliberately* sketchy business idea. Use 5W1H to find why it fails the
[four-question test](../../principles/values.md#the-test-for-any-decision). → Output: the idea
+ the words that exposed it. (Learning to *kill* bad ideas fast is a superpower.)

## Level 3 — Think Like a Skeptic (media + AI literacy)

### Quest 3.1 — Interrogate a claim
Find a bold claim online ("X makes you rich!", a viral headline). Run **Who** benefits from me
believing this? **What** is the counter-argument? **Why** is it being said *now*? **How** do we
know it's true? → Output: is the claim trustworthy? Why or why not?

### Quest 3.2 — Fact-check your AI teammate
Ask an AI ([hello-agent's big brothers](../../../agents/)) a factual question, then 5W1H its
answer: **How** do we know this is true? **What** would prove it wrong? → Output: did the AI get
it right? How did you check? (This is the [*verify, then trust*](../../principles/agentic-engineering.md)
rule in action — the most important habit of an AI-native builder.)

## Level 4 — Make It a Reflex

### Quest 4.1 — Run the agent tool
The same grid is wired into our repo for AI teammates. Run the
[critical-thinking workflow](../../../.claude/workflows/critical-thinking-review.js) on a real
idea and read how six AI agents (one per question-word) interrogate it. → Output: compare the
AI's 5W1H to yours — what did it catch that you missed, and what did *you* catch that *it*
missed?

### Quest 4.2 — Teach David
Play "The Six Detective Words" with your little brother until he can chant them. → Output:
David asks all six about something on his own.

## Why this compounds

Every rung of the [milestone ladder](../../vision/milestones.md) is really a *harder thinking
problem*. The people who climb fastest aren't the ones who work hardest — they're the ones who
**ask the right question first** and don't waste months building the wrong thing. 5W1H is how
you find that question on purpose, every time.
