# 3. Compliance Checklist — complete before physical selling

> This is operational guidance, not legal advice. Call the relevant city business office / 311 or a local attorney for a fact-specific answer.

## 3.1 Non-negotiable
- Obtain **written permission** from the property owner.
- Confirm the operating jurisdiction: Kansas City, Missouri; Kansas City, Kansas; Independence; Overland Park; etc. Rules vary.
- Confirm whether a **business license**, **zoning clearance**, **temporary/event permit**, or **vendor permit** is needed for that exact address and activity.
- Obtain a **Missouri retail sales tax license before retail sales** if operating in Missouri; configure checkout to collect the correct tax.
- Do not sell or give away alcohol or food.
- Do not claim affiliation with tournament organizers, a team, the stadium, event sponsors, or the Fan Festival.
- Do not set up in an access-controlled district or distribute promotional material in restricted event areas.
- Carry general liability coverage if a venue requires it.
- Keep invoice/receipt records and inventory purchase records.

## 3.2 Location rule
Your best legal route is **inside or immediately outside an established, normal operating business** where:
- the operator remains open in its usual location;
- the operator explicitly invites you;
- the activity is a normal local commercial activation—not a stadium-adjacent ambush campaign;
- local licensing/zoning confirms the setup.

**Examples to pitch**
- bar or restaurant watch party
- hotel lobby / patio
- brewery
- coffee shop
- coworking space
- existing retailer with curbside space
- apartment complex hosting a private resident event
- neighborhood market

**Avoid**
- stadium perimeter
- official bus hub
- Fan Festival perimeter
- closed/restricted streets
- public sidewalks without city authorization
- parking lots that have no explicit owner approval
- any site where the only reason for operating there is to intercept event traffic

## 6.9 Stripe implementation
- Use Stripe Checkout for first launch speed.
- Create a Checkout Session with:
  - line items
  - tax behavior set according to the tax configuration you verify
  - metadata: `location_id`, `partner_slug`, `order_type`
- Webhook events:
  - `checkout.session.completed` → mark order paid, reserve/decrement inventory, email QR confirmation
  - `payment_intent.payment_failed` → mark payment failed
  - `charge.refunded` → mark refund and restore inventory
- Rental deposit:
  - safest MVP: collect a **refundable deposit as a separate line item** and process a partial refund upon return
  - do not build complicated authorization-hold mechanics for v1
- Do not store card details.

Buy the starter inventory, run Jun 27 as an instrumented test, and scale for Jul 3.

Increase rain kits—not unrelated inventory. Use preorders and location pickup. Never conduct unapproved street vending just because demand spikes.

# 13. Metrics for the July 3 Scale Decision

Scale only if Jun 27 reaches:
- ≥25 total paid orders OR ≥$1,000 gross revenue
- ≥$30 average order value
- ≥55% gross margin on physical goods
- ≥10% QR scan → order conversion
- ≥2 high-margin digital sales
- ≥1 partner who wants to repeat
- no compliance or property-access issue
- zero serious fulfillment failures

Kill/modify if:
- scans are high but purchase conversion is <3% → simplify offer/pricing
- sales are mostly $7 charging → make charging a bundle upsell, not main product
- customers do not want kits → sell components separately
- traffic exists but venue is wrong → change location, not inventory
- no one books media → run 3 free demo reels with creator partners, then reassess
